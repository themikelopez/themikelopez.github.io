Creating a new brand identity

Working with our design team and clients, we developed & refined a new logo and brand image.

Implementing a design to bring Coops & Feathers into the modern age.

Using the stylesheet from our lead designer, I overhauled the original website to mirror the design.

A collaborative effort, with freedom to work independently

Along with provided assets, I was able to populate the site with beautiful images from Unsplash and created copy to lend itself to the brand and marketing goals of the client.

About The Work

With this contract, I was given the reigns to lead in design implementation and to help collaborate on the redesign and re-branding. Working with the team at BlueSpruce, we completely reinvented Innovation Pet into Coops & Feathers. Their Shopify e-commerce site needed to reflect these major changes and showcase their new brand identity to pivot from a provider of a wide variety of pet products to a more refined, core foundation of products to represent the new direction of Coops & Feathers.

Communicating often with the client to make sure the site was honed to reflect exactly what they were working for, we were able to rapidly and efficiently deliver a new website that was ready to start taking orders immediately.

In the final phases of the project, the clients desired to add a pre-order feature to the site. I took lead on this and began implementing the feature through the process of researching and vetting different apps that would be compatible with Shopify. Unfortunately, the app I originally had hoped would work with our theme did not; however, it was a valuable learning experience.

During the troubleshooting process, I had to contact the development team for that particular pre-order app and attempt to find a solution together. Working with their support team and developers, we made an effort to work our way through the app's failure and determine where we were running into issues.

In the end, we were not able to successfully get the initial app to integrate, so I ultimately went with another app that worked with our theme template and was able to deliver for the client.
